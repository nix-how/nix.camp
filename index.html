Nix Camp

Nix Camp 2025 is an 8 day event from August 27th to September 3rd. A time to come together and learn about Nix whilst living near to the Welsh/British countryside, surrounded by nature and friends. There are no formal talks, training sessions or workshops; though attendees are encouraged to share, collaborate and give informal talks to eachother.

This is the fifth Nix Camp.

Tickets/Booking

The Nix Camp 2025 dates are August 27th until September 3rd. This year, Nix Camp will consist of two parts and span two venues.

Ticket option 1 is priced at £150 per person. This covers attendance and food, but not accomodation.

Ticket option 2 is priced at £300 per person This covers attendance, but also accomodation at the AstralShip Venue.

Both ticket options expect attendees to find their own accomodation whilst in Liverpool/Birkenhead If you can't make it to the full 8 days, visitors are welcome, just drop in and contribute what you can!

Nix Camp is proudly using the NixOS module for pretix with stripe integration to manage ticketing this year, as well as the simple-nixos-mailserver for mail services: 

services.pretix.enable = true
mailserver.enable = true

Venue & Accommodation

Part 1 of Nix.Camp 2025 will be hosted at BirkenHack in Birkenhead, very close to Liverpool, 27 - 30 August

Part 2 of Nix.Camp 2025 will be hosted at Astralship in Deiniolen, Wales, 31st August - 3rd September

Part 1

First, 3 days of intense hacking at BirkenHack in Birkenhead, Hamilton Square, with lots of ethernet. Where attendees are expected to find their own accomodation in Birkenhead, or Liverpool which is close by. Trains to and from Liverpool are frequent and available until 11:40PM, and there are plenty of things to see on the Wirral Peninsula. We will visit DoES Liverpool (another local co-working/hackspace), Hilbre Island and Birkenhead Park

Brunswick Court, Price Street, Birkenhead, Hamilton Square CH41 6LW
Hamilton Square is a town square in Birkenhead, Wirral, England. The Georgian square, which was designed by Edinburgh architect James Gillespie Graham, has the most Grade I listed buildings outside London (after Trafalgar Square). It is named after the family of the wife of Scottish shipbuilder William Laird.

Part 2

Secondly, we will spend 5 days at the Astral Ship where 8 rooms are available for on-site accomodation, and are covered by the more expensive ticket option, which is limited in supply. We will head for the Astral Ship venue on the 30th, and sleep there that night, in case you have booked the accomodation ticket option.

Accommodation will be provided by the Astralship, a hackspace that promotes creativity, collaboration and innovation located in Deiniolen, Wales. Festri Cefn Y Waun, Astral Road, Deiniolen, Caernarfon LL55 3NB

The Astralship is based in a 200 year old chapel, where the mountains of Snowdonia meet the sea. The chapel dates from 1838 and is located in an unbelievably beautiful area, with endless mountains, beaches and quarries to explore. It's the perfect place to be if you like hiking, climbing and wild swimming.

There are many AirBNB and hotel offerings available in the surrounding area if this arrangement does not suit you, or full accomodation tickets are out of stock.

Food will still be provided to those who choose not to stay overnight at the venue, as this cost is included in the ticket price.
